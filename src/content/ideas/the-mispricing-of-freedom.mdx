## The Blind Spot

Here is a number that should alarm anyone who cares about the future of civilization: **less than 1% of U.S. charitable giving goes to liberty and institutional quality.**

Americans give over $500 billion annually to charity. Of that, a fraction of a fraction reaches the organizations defending the institutions that make all other giving possible. We fund hospitals, universities, arts programs, and environmental causes, all worthy. But we systematically neglect the foundational infrastructure upon which every one of those causes depends: **freedom itself.**

This is not just an oversight. It is a mispricing of catastrophic proportions.

## Freedom as Infrastructure

Consider: what makes a hospital function? Not just doctors and equipment, but a rule of law that protects property rights, enforces contracts, and ensures transparent governance. What allows a university to pursue truth? Not just endowments, but academic freedom protected by democratic institutions.

Freedom is not one cause among many. **It is the substrate upon which all causes depend.**

When Daron Acemoglu and James Robinson received the Nobel Prize for demonstrating that robust institutions create 20%+ GDP growth over 25 years, they quantified what freedom advocates have long known: liberty is not just a moral good. It is an economic multiplier.

## The Case for Freedom Alpha

Observable democratic and liberty outcomes improve market performance over time. Family offices can identify and invest in what I call **"freedom alpha"**: the measurable returns generated by advancing institutional quality and democratic governance.

This is not philanthropy as we know it. This is strategic investment in the conditions that create prosperity.

The proposal is simple but radical: add "F" to ESG. Freedom metrics (rule of law indices, press freedom rankings, democratic governance scores) should inform investment decisions just as environmental, social, and governance factors do.

## A Call to Action

We are not losing because autocrats are stronger. We are losing because they are better organized, better resourced, and largely unopposed.

If we want to defeat dictators and reclaim the future of freedom, we must stop reacting and start investing.

The moonshot goal: **grow freedom funding from less than 1% to 10% of U.S. philanthropy within a decade.** This is achievable. It requires only that we recognize what we already know: freedom is the best investment humanity has ever made.
